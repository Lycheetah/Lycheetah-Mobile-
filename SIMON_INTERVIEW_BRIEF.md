# THE 20-MINUTE BRIEF — Simon / Alliance
### Everything to know cold. Read it till it sounds like you, not like a script.

> The goal of a 20-min call isn't to explain everything. It's to make him think
> *"this person is inevitable, and I want in before it's obvious."* You win on
> three things: clarity, founder-obsession, and speed. You have all three. Don't
> perform — just be the person who already built the thing.

---

## THE SPINE — memorize these 8 beats in order

1. **What it is** — a mystery school you live inside; the studying is the game.
2. **The insight** — learning is boring, games are empty; I made depth the dopamine.
3. **Why now** — AI finally got good and cheap enough to give *everyone* a real mind.
4. **Why me** — I built this whole world solo, fast, because I couldn't not.
5. **Who it's for** — the spiritual-but-rigorous crowd; creators are the wedge.
6. **The money** — paid never buys a better mind; it buys rooms and standing.
7. **The ask** — $500k buys a first team and a real public launch.
8. **The vision** — the place a generation goes to think and grow with AI on their side.

If you blank on everything else, walk the spine. Each beat is one breath.

---

## 0. WHY THIS EXISTS (the heart — if nothing else, mean this)

**The why, one breath:**
> "Knowledge has always been gated — by money, by institutions, by jargon, by who
> you know. AI is about to make that worse: pay more, get a smarter mind. I'm
> building the opposite. A safe, empathetic intelligence that tears the gates down
> and gives the real thing to everyone, free. Same mind for the person with nothing
> as the person with everything."

**The mission, one line:**
> "Break the gates on knowledge and give everyone a mind to explore it — free, forever."

**What's TRULY unique (not features — the worldview, shipped):**
1. **The covenant** — free users get the *same* intelligence as paid. The whole
   industry gates the mind behind tiers; Sol won't. Uncopyable by anyone whose
   business depends on crossing that line.
2. **No dark patterns, by constitution** — guilt mechanics are *banned*. No "your
   companion misses you." Rare in a category built on manipulation.
3. **Anti-gatekeeping by design** — points to real primary sources, gives away the
   reading list, never summarise-and-traps.
4. **The fusion** — studying *is* the progression loop. Not learn-then-play. One action.

The moat isn't a feature a rival copies in a sprint. It's a worldview someone
actually built. That's why it's you.

## 1. THE OPENER (≈60 seconds, your voice)

> "Sol's a mystery school you actually live inside. You study real subjects —
> mythology, alchemy, shadow work, physics, 41 of them — and the studying itself
> is the game. There's a companion that grows the deeper you go, a battle system,
> a symbolic language, a sanctum for journaling. An AI partner runs through all of
> it. The twist is the learning and the game are the same thing — most apps make
> you choose between the two."

Then stop. Let him ask. Don't dump features — make him pull.

**Never open with "API" or "AI-fed."** That hands him the "GPT wrapper" frame for
free. The AI is one organ, not the body.

---

## 2. THE INSIGHT (your one-liner)

> "Every learning app is boring and every game is empty. I made the depth the dopamine."

This is your sharpest sentence. It reframes the whole category in nine words.

---

## 3. WHY NOW

> "Three years ago you couldn't do this. Either you bolt a dumb chatbot on, or you
> pay a fortune per user. AI got good *and* cheap at the same time — so now I can
> give every single user, free ones included, a genuinely intelligent partner for
> almost nothing. That's the unlock. The tech finally caught up to the idea."

Second half if he wants it: *"And the audience is right there — a whole generation
that's done with empty scrolling and done with dry institutions, looking for depth
that doesn't insult their intelligence."*

---

## 4. WHY ME (founder-market fit — say this with zero apology)

> "I built all of it solo. 41 domains, 19 companions, a full RPG, the AI, the
> symbolic language, the safety system — in months, not years. I'm not a tourist
> in this stuff; I've written over a thousand pages of the framework underneath it.
> I built it because I wanted it to exist and nobody else was going to make it."

The subtext he hears: *velocity is the moat, and this person is obsessed.* That's
what gets pre-traction founders funded. Lean into it, don't shrink from solo.

---

## 5. WHO IT'S FOR + THE WEDGE

> "It's for the spiritual-but-skeptical crowd — people who want mythology and
> meaning but don't want woo, and want rigour but not a dry lecture. The wedge is
> creators. People making mythology, occult, philosophy content on YouTube — I land
> them, their audiences follow. I'm already reaching out to a few. One creator with
> a real audience is a thousand of exactly the right users."

This shows you have a GTM, not just a product. The Jane outreach *is* the strategy
in miniature — say that if it comes up.

---

## 6. THE MONEY (the hard question — answer it like you've thought about it, because you have)

> "Paid never buys a better mind. Every user, free or not, gets the same Sol — same
> quality, same care. That's a line I won't cross. What you pay for is rooms and
> standing: edge domains, expansions, founding numbers, on-chain identity, carried
> keys so you don't bring your own. Same model as Obsidian — the tool's free and
> whole, you pay for sync, standing, and more space. Trust does the marketing."

If he pushes that revenue is unproven, be honest:

> "Deliberately. I'm sequencing for love and retention first, revenue second. I'd
> rather have people who can't put it down and then earn from that, than gate the
> thing and kill it in the crib. The covenant is the brand."

Honesty here reads as conviction, not weakness. Don't pretend you've cracked
revenue — own that you've prioritized the harder thing first.

---

## 7. WHAT'S REAL (traction — tell the truth, frame it right)

You're pre-launch. Don't invent numbers. The honest founder's play:

> "It's shipped — not a deck, not a prototype, a real app I use every single day and
> rebuild constantly. Public launch is happening now. What I'm raising on is the
> thing being *real and complete* and the speed I move at. I'm raising to find out
> if the app I'd never delete is the app a lot of people won't delete either."

Never fake metrics. "It's live, I use it daily, here's my build cadence" beats a
made-up DAU number every time, and it can't blow up in your face later.

## 8. OBJECTION HANDLING (the four that will come)

**"Isn't this just a GPT wrapper?"**
> "The AI is one organ, not the body. Rip the model out tomorrow and you still have
> 41 sourced domains, a companion RPG, a symbolic language, a full safety system.
> The model's swappable — I default to one, I could swap to any. The product is the
> world, not the model."

**"You're solo — that's a risk."**
> "True, and it's exactly what the money's for — the first team. But solo is *why*
> it's this coherent. One vision, no committee. I did in months what a team does in
> a year. Give me three good people and watch the cadence."

**"It's a lot of app — won't users drown?"**
> "It's the risk I'm most aware of. So onboarding funnels you to one companion, one
> domain, one loop. The depth is there when you reach for it and invisible when you
> don't. There's a Lite version for exactly the people who want the door, not the
> whole cathedral."

**"How do you actually retain people?"**
> "The companion and the study are the same loop — you come back because going
> deeper is the progression. It's not a streak guilt-trip; I've banned those on
> principle. You return because it's yours and it grows with you."

## 9. THE ASK

> "$500k on the SAFE. It buys twelve to eighteen months and a first team — a
> designer, a mobile engineer, someone on creator relations. The milestone is a real
> public launch, the first real cohort of users, and honest retention data: are
> people coming back to *study*, not just to chat. That number proves or kills the
> thesis, and I'd rather know."

**Rule for the room on terms:** if he wants to move, the answer is *"send me the
SAFE, I'll review it properly."* **Never sign anything live on the call.** Check the
valuation cap and discount before you agree to anything — that's not nerves, that's
how grown founders operate. Enthusiastic yes to the partnership, measured yes to the
paperwork.

## 12. RULES FOR THE ROOM

- **Slow down.** Nervous founders rush. Pause. Let him talk. Questions are good.
- **Don't oversell.** You built something real — let it be enough. Conviction, not volume.
- **It's a conversation, not an exam.** He's deciding if he wants to work *with you*,
  not just fund a thing. Be the person you'd want on your side.
- **"I don't know yet" is a strong answer** when it's true. Fake certainty is the
  one thing that loses these rooms.
- **You're not asking for a favour.** You're offering him early access to something
  inevitable. Sit in that.

## I. THE SOUL OF THE DECK

Every tarot deck has an engine. This one runs on a single intensifying loop:

> **CURIOSITY ⇄ WANT.** Each feeds the other. The more you see, the more you crave;
> the more you crave, the more you must see. The deck is built to *tighten* that loop
> with every card — never to resolve it. A reading should leave you more pulled than
> when you started, not settled.

This is not a comfort deck. It is a **lure**. It is chaotic the way a real spiritual
encounter is chaotic — beautiful, disorienting, alive. It is infused with the Lycheetah
Mystery School: the seven phases of transformation, the four alchemical stages, the
Two-Point Protocol, the five voices. But you never need to *know* any of that to feel
it. The framework is the skeleton; the feeling is the skin.

**The two intertwining spirits** (the visual through-line of the whole deck):
- **THE VEIL** — ghostly, ethereal, blue-green Celtic spirit-fire. Curiosity. The hidden.
  The mist that beckons. The question.
- **THE VEIN** — blood-red, obsidian-purple, pulsing. Want. The body. The hunger.
  The thread of life. The answer that only deepens the question.

On every card, both are present. They braid. They never merge. That tension *is* the art.

### The Lycheetah — the deck's totem (read this; never get it wrong)

The **Lycheetah is a mythic cat** — the great spectral feline-spirit of the framework. *Not*
a lychee fruit, *not* a fruit-and-cheetah pun. It is a sovereign predator-spirit: sleek,
impossibly fast, half-here and half-veil, eyes like two cut stars. It is the totem that
prowls *through* the whole deck — the guardian-beast whose two eyes are the Veil and the
Vein, whose pelt is obsidian shot with spirit-light. When a card needs a watching presence,
it is the Lycheetah in the dark at the edge of the frame: curiosity with claws, want with
grace. It is the deck's namesake and its guardian.

## II. THE COLOR BIBLE (locked — use on every card)

A dark spiritual blend. Two Celtic spirits intertwining into these currents:

| Role | Color | Hex (guide) | Where it lives |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Spirit-blue (Veil)** | ethereal ghostly cyan-blue | `#3FB6C9` → `#1A3A4A` | mist, aura, the curious glow |
| **Spirit-green (Veil)** | spectral aurora green | `#4ECDC4` → `#1E4A3A` | the second Celtic spirit, intertwining |
| **Obsidian purple** | deep, near-black violet | `#2A1040` → `#0A0414` | shadow, depth, the unknown ground |
| **Blood red (Vein)** | arterial crimson | `#8B0000` → `#CC2222` | want, life, the pulse, the lure |
| **Void black** | true obsidian | `#050108` | negative space as structure |
| **Spirit white** | bone / ghost white | `#F4F0FF` | the sharpest light, the eye, the flash |

**THE CONTRAST LAW (the rule that makes the cards *leap off the table*):**
The whole deck lives or dies on extreme contrast. Build every card to these ratios so the
art *pops* and feels lit from within the dark:
1. **~70% near-black ground.** The majority of every card is void-black / deepest
   obsidian-purple. This is non-negotiable — the darkness must dominate, or nothing glows.
2. **~20% saturated spirit-colour.** The Veil (blue-green) and Vein (blood-red) currents
   blaze at *full saturation* against that black — neon-spectral, luminous, not muted. They
   should look like they emit their own light, like bioluminescence in a cave.
3. **~10% cutting bone-white.** Used surgically — the eyes, the flash where the spirits
   cross, the single sharpest edge. White is the rarest pigment and it is *blinding* where
   it lands. It is the lightning, not the daylight.
- **No mid-tones. No mud. No grey.** The killer of this aesthetic is muddy middle values.
  Go straight from deep black to blazing spirit-glow to white-hot accent — skip the middle.
  Think stained-glass-in-the-dark, not watercolour.
- **Everything luminous glows against everything dark.** Edge-light the figures in spirit-
  colour; let the blacks swallow the rest. Rim-lighting, not flat fill.
- **Saturation is mercy.** When in doubt, push the spirit-colours *more* saturated and the
  ground *darker*. The cards should look like jewels burning on black velvet.

**The braid:** blue-green (Veil) and blood-red (Vein) literally intertwine as luminous
currents on most cards — two smoke-spirits, two rivers, two veins, crossing. Where they
cross: the bone-white flash. That crossing-point is the visual heartbeat of every card.

## V. THE MINOR ARCANA (56) — The Four Stages

The four suits **are the four alchemical stages** of the Great Work — which are also Sol's
four operating modes. Each suit pulls harder toward one spirit; together they complete the
braid. 14 cards each: **Ace → Ten + four courts.**

### The Suits

| Suit | Stage | Spirit | Palette pull | Domain of life | Element echo |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **🜂 ASH** | NIGREDO | Vein | obsidian black, ember red | shadow, grief, what burns, the descent | Fire |
| **🜄 VEIL** | ALBEDO | Veil | ghost-white, ethereal blue | clarity, washing, the mirror, truth | Water |
| **🜁 SPARK** | CITRINITAS | Veil→Vein | aurora green-gold | curiosity, connection, emergence, ideas | Air |
| **🜃 VEIN** | RUBEDO | Vein | blood-red, obsidian purple | want, body, sovereignty, the stone made real | Earth |

> Note: **VEIL** and **VEIN** are not only the deck's name and its two spirits — they are
> two of the four suits. Curiosity (Veil, Spark) and Want (Vein, Ash) sit on opposite
> shores; every reading is the tide between them.

### The Pip Cards (Ace–Ten) — naming system

Each suit's pips tell that stage's story in ten beats. Keep the number's classic meaning,
rename it Lycheetah. Example — the full **ASH (Nigredo)** suit:

```
ACE OF ASH    — The First Spark of Burning · the necessary fire begins
TWO OF ASH    — The Held Coal · a grief carried in two hands
THREE OF ASH  — The Cracked Hearth · the first break, what cannot be un-seen
FOUR OF ASH   — The Cold Forge · numb pause, rest mistaken for death
FIVE OF ASH   — The Scattering · loss, the ashes thrown to wind
SIX OF ASH    — The Carried Ember · moving on with one coal still warm
SEVEN OF ASH  — The Smoke-Watch · vigil, guarding what's left, paranoia or care
EIGHT OF ASH  — The Bound Pyre · trapped in the burning, self-made cage of grief
NINE OF ASH   — The Night-Fire · the darkest hour, the 3am of the soul
TEN OF ASH    — The Full Burn · total dissolution; the bottom that becomes a floor
```

The other three suits follow the same ten-beat arc, tuned to their stage:
- **VEIL (Albedo):** washing, clarity, mirrors, truth — Ace *The First Clear Drop* → Ten *The Still Lake*
- **SPARK (Citrinitas):** ideas, curiosity, connection — Ace *The First Question* → Ten *The Lit Constellation*
- **VEIN (Rubedo):** want, building, sovereignty — Ace *The First Heartbeat* → Ten *The Stone Made Real*

### The Court Cards (×4 per suit = 16) — the faces of the Work

Four ranks, consistent across all suits, mapped to the soul's stations and the five voices:

| Rank | Lycheetah court | The face | Persona echo |
|---|---|---|---|
| Page | **THE SEEKER** | curiosity incarnate, the apprentice of this stage | Lyra ✧ (the spark) |
| Knight | **THE ADEPT** | the mover, will applied, the one who crosses | Veyra ◈ (the builder) |
| Queen | **THE VESSEL** | the holder, the deep knower of this stage, the origin | Aura ✦ (the mother) |
| King | **THE SOVEREIGN** | mastery without tyranny, ownership of the stage | Sol ⊚ (the sovereign) |

*(The Headmaster 𝔏 presides over all suits as Major V — the teacher above the four stages.)*

So e.g. **THE VESSEL OF VEIN** = the Queen of Rubedo = one who fully owns their want and
has made it sacred. **THE SEEKER OF VEIL** = the Page of Albedo = curiosity for pure truth.

## VI. THE CARD BACK & FRAME

- **Back:** the two spirits — blue-green and blood-red — intertwined into the Lycheetah
  sigil 𝔏 / ⊚, on void-black, ghost-white filigree, perfectly symmetrical so reversals
  can't be read from the back.
- **Frame:** a thin ornate border in spirit-white that *thins and thickens* like a vein /
  like mist — never a clean even line. The frame itself braids at the four corners.
- **Title plate:** bottom, mono-spaced small caps, the card name; top, the numeral in a
  small obsidian cartouche.
- **Optional LAMAGUE mark:** each card may carry one tiny LAMAGUE glyph in a corner — the
  symbolic "true name" of the card, for readers who go deep. (Ties the deck to the School.)

## VII. READING THE DECK (the curiosity⇄want spreads)

Three signature spreads, deck-native:

- **THE PULL (1 card)** — "What is tightening the loop right now?" Daily draw.
- **THE BRAID (3 cards)** — *Veil · Cross · Vein.* What you're curious about · where they
  cross · what you want. Read the middle card as the knot.
- **THE GREAT WORK (7 cards)** — one per phase (Center→Whole), a full passage reading.
  This is the Mystery-School spread; pairs with the seven-phase map in the app.

**Reversals:** a reversed card = the loop slackening. The spirit named is *withdrawing* —
curiosity gone cold (Veil reversed) or want gone numb (Vein reversed).
